**Position**: None (watchlist) | **Watch since**: 2026-07-14 | **Ref price at creation**: ₹2,641

## Thesis as of 2026-07-14

Asian Paints is a dented-but-working moat available at the wrong price. The brand + dealer-distribution moat held ROE above 21% and gross margin at 43% through Birla Opus's ₹10,000 Cr assault — evidence of durability, not decay. But three years of flat revenue (~1% CAGR FY23–26) and a 58x TTM multiple mean the market is pricing an intact moat while the business shows a dented one. **This is a conditional-entry thesis**: buy near ₹2,000 (≈34x TTM EPS, base-case ~15%/yr forward return) OR on hard evidence the price war is ending — whichever comes first. Not before.

## Falsifiers (for the watch thesis)

1. **Grasim escalates**: fresh capital committed to Birla Opus beyond the original ~₹10,000 Cr → industry margin reset is structural, abandon the entry plan
2. **Gross margin breaks**: Asian Paints GM falls below 38% for two consecutive quarters → the moat is no longer absorbing the attack
3. **Share loss accelerates**: decorative share falls below ~45% → distribution moat failing, not just repricing
4. **ROE below 15%** in any full year → business quality thesis wrong
5. **JSW Paints scales as second funded attacker** (>5% share) → duopoly stabilisation scenario dead; three-way wars don't rationalise

## Entry triggers (either → run /investment-checklist again)

- Price ≤ ₹2,000, OR
- Birla Opus announces price increases / Grasim commentary shifts from "share gain" to "profitability", OR
- Two consecutive quarters of ≥8% revenue growth
