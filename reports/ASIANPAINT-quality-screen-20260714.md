## What the Failed Filter Means

Revenue has been flat for three years: ₹34,489 → 35,495 → 33,906 → 35,584 Cr. Two forces, both structural:

1. **Birla Opus entry (2024)** — Grasim's ₹10,000 Cr assault on the paint market. Asian Paints' decorative market share has eroded from ~59% toward low-50s. Price competition compressed FY25 OPM to 18% from 21%.
2. **Urban demand slowdown** — paint demand tracks housing and discretionary renovation; both were soft FY25–26.

**The other side**: FY26 revenue (+5%) and FCF (₹5,604 Cr, the best in 4 years) suggest stabilisation. ROE at 21.8% through the worst competitive attack in the company's history is evidence of a real moat. The market has already repriced: P/E 60.8x TTM is still not cheap, but the stock is ~8% below its 52-week high while Birla Opus's losses mount.

**Watch triggers**: two consecutive quarters of ≥8% revenue growth, or evidence Birla Opus is retreating from price war → re-run this screen.
